## Bottom line

The highest-leverage idea that respects all three axioms is **A: port OpenSpace's WORKFLOW-vs-REFERENCE consumption framing into the doctrine/mandate** (`registry.py:614-629`) — a pure in-generation text edit, costs the hook nothing, and attacks false-SKIPPING where it lives by converting the emitted `USING` token from a label into an instruction to *execute the skill's verified steps*. Pair it with **B**, binding the line-1 `USING /x` to a named first action (`registry.py:688-704`), and optionally **F**, collapsing the top-k menu to a single decisive pick when the retrieval margin is high (`registry.py:340-504`) to close the "none of these five fit → skip" dodge the mental-model already diagnoses (`mental-model.md:68-74`). **Reject** the analyzer (D, post-hoc = anti-caveman, `mental-model.md:168-176`), delegation (E, violates no-execution), the success-rate preview (C, blocked on the open ADR-0009 score↔adoption re-measurement), and above all do not copy OpenSpace's "when in doubt leave it out" polarity, which is inverted for a system that executes and would break skill-concierge's burden-of-proof-on-SKIP doctrine (`ADR-0015:34-37`).
