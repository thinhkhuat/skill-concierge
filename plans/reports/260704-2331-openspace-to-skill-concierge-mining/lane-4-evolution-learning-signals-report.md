## Bottom line

Two concepts genuinely survive the domain gap, and both are **re-interpretations of metrics skill-concierge already has**, not new machinery. (1) OpenSpace's metric-monitor diagnosis "selected-but-not-applied ⇒ the instructions mis-sell the skill" (`evolver.py:1573-1579`) is the highest-value port: it tells skill-concierge that its ADR-0011 chronic never-take cohort should also be surfaced as a **description-repair flag**, not just silently dropped from the menu — same metric, an added SUGGEST output where a human acts. (2) The "structural-pattern quality scorer" concept (`README.md:35`) validates promoting `build_triggers.py`'s existing empty-trigger detection (`build_triggers.py:99-112`) plus ADR-0016's decision-section awareness into a first-class **retrievability linter** that flags thin/section-less descriptions. Everything else — the entire evolver/patch code-mutation engine, all three FIX/DERIVED/CAPTURED authoring modes, the tool-degradation trigger, the CAPTURED new-skill path, and 2 of the 3 metric-monitor health rules — deliberately left out because it requires authoring/executing/versioning skills or mining a per-task execution recording, none of which a selection-only tool does. That is roughly **85–90% of the evolution engine out of scope**; the residual value is a signal/suggestion layer (concierge flags, human or skill-creator fixes), never an evolution loop.

## Concerns / unresolved

- The "structural-pattern scorer" (row 2) is a README claim with **no verified implementation** in the files read — treat it as directional inspiration, not a design to replicate.
- Even the HIGH-tier port (row 1) reuses the SAME `_offer_conversion` metric ADR-0011 already flags as anti-correlated with usefulness (ADR-0009); the port is a new *reading* of that metric, so its precision is only as good as that underlying signal.
